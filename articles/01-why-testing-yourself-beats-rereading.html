You've been rereading wrong your whole life

I want to say something that will feel uncomfortable if your entire study system is built around it. Rereading your notes does almost nothing for your long term memory. Not less than ideal. Almost nothing.

I have sat across from hundreds of students who were doing everything right on paper. Highlighted textbook. Color coded notes. Hours logged. And still walking into exams feeling like the material slipped through their hands the second they needed it. Every one of them was rereading. None of them were testing themselves.

Here is what I learned after over ten years of working with students: the amount of time you study matters far less than what you do with that time. And the single biggest lever, by a wide margin, is testing yourself instead of rereading.

Rereading feels productive because recognizing something on a page is easy. You see it, you nod, it feels familiar, you move on. But recognizing information and being able to produce it from memory are two completely different skills. And the one you actually need, in an exam room or standing next to a patient, is the second one.

So here is what I want you to do instead.

Close the book. Actually close it. Then try to explain the concept out loud, or write it down from memory, before you look at your notes again. You will get things wrong. Good. That is the point. Every time you struggle to pull something out of your own head and then check it, you are building a stronger, more retrievable memory than any amount of passive review could give you.

I tell every student the same thing. If studying never feels a little uncomfortable, you are probably not doing the part that works.

A five minute way to start today

  1. Read or watch something once, just enough to get the shape of it.
  2. Close everything.
  3. Say out loud or write down everything you remember.
  4. Go back and check. Pay more attention to what you missed than what you got right.
  5. Come back to the same material in a day or two and try again.

That is it. You do not need a new app or a new system to start. You need to stop reading the answer before you have tried to find it yourself.

I have watched students transform their exam performance with nothing more than this one shift. Not more hours. Not a better textbook. Just the willingness to close the book and try.
