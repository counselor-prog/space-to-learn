Where is your time going?

Most students feel behind, overwhelmed, or like there is never enough time. Before changing anything, it helps to see clearly where your hours are actually going. Not where you think they go. Where they actually go. This tool walks you through that honestly, step by step.

Work through each section in order. It takes about ten minutes and most people find the results surprising.

Step 1

Your 24 hours

On a typical day, where does your time go? Enter how many hours you spend on each activity. The circle fills in as you go. The total must add up to 24.

Total: 0 / 24 hours
Step 2

Your week in hours

There are 168 hours in every week. Enter how many hours per week you spend on each activity. The calculator will show your total and how many hours are left over.

Your total 0 hrs
Free hours per week (168 minus your total) 168 hrs
Step 3

Find your actual screen time

Most students underestimate how much time they spend on social media. Your phone already tracks this for you. Here is exactly how to find it, whether you have an iPhone or an Android.

  • 1
    Open the Settings app on your iPhone (the grey icon with gears).
  • 2
    Scroll down and tap Screen Time.
  • 3
    If Screen Time is not turned on yet, tap Turn On Screen Time and follow the prompts. It will start tracking from that point forward.
  • 4
    Tap See All Activity at the top of the Screen Time page.
  • 5
    At the top, switch from Day to Week to see your weekly totals.
  • 6
    Scroll down to see time broken down by app and category. Social media apps appear under Social Networking.
  • 7
    Note your total weekly social media hours and add them into your weekly breakdown above under Social media and phone.
Tip: If someone else set up Screen Time with a passcode you don't know, go to Settings, Screen Time, and tap "Change Screen Time Passcode" to reset it.
  • 1
    Open the Settings app on your Android phone.
  • 2
    Search for Digital Wellbeing in the search bar at the top, or scroll to find it. On Samsung devices it may be called Digital Wellbeing and Parental Controls.
  • 3
    Tap Digital Wellbeing. You will see a dashboard with a chart showing your usage.
  • 4
    Tap the chart or the Dashboard option to see time broken down by app.
  • 5
    To see weekly totals, look for a Week tab or toggle at the top of the dashboard. On some Samsung devices, swipe the chart left.
  • 6
    Find your social media apps (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Snapchat, etc.) and note how many hours per week you are spending on each one.
  • 7
    Add your total weekly social media hours into your weekly breakdown above under Social media and phone.
Tip: If you do not see Digital Wellbeing, your device may use a different name. Try searching "screen time" or "usage" in Settings. Older Android versions may not have this feature built in, but you can download the Google Family Link or ActionDash app for the same information.
Step 4

Are you a procrastinator?

Answer each question honestly. There are no right or wrong answers. Select the option that best describes your usual behaviour, not your best week.

Never = 1    Occasionally = 2    Often = 3    Always = 4

Step 5

What actually matters to you?

Now that you know where your time goes, think about what is most important in your life right now. List your top 10 priorities in order, from most to least important. Be honest, not aspirational.

How well does the time you spend each week match your priorities? What do you need to add, reduce, or remove from your week to better match what matters most? What will you add to your week? What will you reduce or stop?
