Mental health resources for Vancouver & BC

Most people find this page one of two ways. Either something is already feeling urgent, or they're the kind of person who likes to know where the exits are before they need them. Either way, you're in the right place. Below is a real, current list of supports. Nothing here requires a referral unless noted, and most of it is free.

If you are in crisis right now: call or text 988, the national suicide crisis helpline, available 24/7. If there is immediate danger to your life, call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room.

Crisis lines, BC-wide

988 Suicide Crisis Helpline
Call or text 988. Available 24/7 across Canada.
310 Mental Health Support Line
Call 310-6789. No area code needed. Free, 24/7 emotional support and referrals anywhere in BC.
Crisis Centre of BC
Call, text, or chat online. Serves the Lower Mainland and connects to the BC-wide crisis network. crisiscentre.bc.ca
1-800-SUICIDE
1-800-784-2433. Provincial suicide prevention line, 24/7.

Culturally specific support

KUU-US Crisis Line Society
1-800-588-8717 (toll free, 24/7). Crisis support by and for Indigenous people across BC, adult/elder and child/youth lines available.
Hope for Wellness Help Line
1-855-242-3310, 24/7. Free counselling for Indigenous peoples across Canada, available in English, French, and on request Cree, Ojibway, and Inuktitut.
Métis Crisis Line
1-833-638-4722, 24/7 support for Métis individuals in BC.
Indian Residential School Survivors Crisis Line
1-800-721-0066, 24/7 emotional support for survivors and family.
VictimLinkBC
1-800-563-0808, 24/7, multilingual, for anyone affected by crime or violence.

Free, structured programs (no referral needed)

BounceBack BC
A free, CMHA-run skill building program for low mood, mild to moderate depression, stress, or anxiety. Delivered by phone or online with a coach.
Foundry BC
Free mental health, substance use, and wellness support for ages 12 to 24. In-person centres across BC or virtually through the Foundry BC app. foundrybc.ca
Wellness Together Canada
Free national platform with self-guided courses, community support, and access to counsellors.
Here to Help BC
A partnership of BC agencies providing plain-language information on mental health and substance use. heretohelp.bc.ca

Vancouver & Lower Mainland specific

Vancouver Coastal Health mental health services
Regional intake for community mental health and substance use services.
Fraser Health mental health services
Covers Surrey and the Fraser Health region, relevant if you are based at the Surrey campus.
211 BC
Call 211 or visit 211.ca for referrals to food, housing, financial, and mental health supports across BC, available in over 240 languages.

Last checked: July 2026. Crisis line numbers do change. If something here looks out of date, please let us know so we can correct it promptly.
