Poetry

I write poetry as a hobby — not for any particular reason, just because sometimes there are things on the mind that don't fit anywhere else. These are some of them.

The Locked Door
He loved his own sadness more than he could love me. Kept it close like an old coat, wore it even in summer. I stood at the door some nights, just wanting to hold his hand. He was inside, talking to himself about the same old wound, turning it over and over like it was the only thing he knew how to hold. I never left in anger. I left because I got tired of loving someone who was already loved — just not by me. Sometimes a person needs to leave their own house before anyone else can move in.
The Faithful Traitor
No one signed the papers, no one raised a hand and said yes, I'll take the weight of mornings, yes, I'll learn what loss means. Yet here we are, enlisted in a war we didn't declare, issued skin and hunger and the particular ache of knowing we are here. The irony is not that it's hard. The irony is that it continues — that the body, that faithful traitor, shows up every day without being asked. And we, its reluctant passengers, watch the road go by and call it living because there is no other word for it.
